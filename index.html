Analysis of the Stroop task

Student project by Hilary Seselja | Udacity Data Analyst Nanodegree

Background Information

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participantâ€™s task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

1. Identify the variables in the experiment

The independent variable is word / color congruency. The ink color was changed from matching the word (congruent condition) to not matching the word (incongruent condition).

The dependent variable is task completion time. This was defined as the amount of time it took participants to name all the ink colors in each task.

2. Establish hypotheses and a statistical test

I expect it will take participants longer to name ink colors in the incongruent condition than in the congruent one.

Null Hypothesis

Changing the display of color words from congruent to incongruent colors will not alter task completion time. The mean difference between the two samples will equal zero.

H0: μD ≤ 0
where μD is the mean difference between the two samples.

Alternative Hypothesis

Changing the display of color words from congruent to incongruent colors will increase task completion time. The mean difference will be greater than zero.

HA: μD > 0

Statistical test: dependent samples t-test

I will test the null hypothesis with a one-tailed dependent samples t-test in the positive direction. α = 0.05.

A dependent samples t-test is appropriate because the experiment has a repeated measures design. The same group participated in two tasks with one condition changed. This provides dependent samples.

Assumptions of the dependent samples t-test.
  • The data type is continuous rather than discrete.
  • The sample is randomly selected.
  • The sample distribution is approximately normal.
  • The sample does not contain extreme outliers.

For this experiment a one-tailed test is appropriate because I am hypothesising a specific directional effect (that applying the incongruent condition will increase task completion time).

α = 0.05 indicates the mean difference will be significant at p < 0.05.

The dependent samples t-test provided evidence that changing the display of color words from congruent to incongruent colors increases task completion time. The t-critical value is 1.714 and the t-statistic is 8.02.

There was a significant difference in the time taken to complete the task in the congruent (M = 14.05, SD = 3.56) and incongruent (M = 22.06, SD = 4.80) conditions; t(23) = 8.02, p < 0.0001, one-tailed. 95% CI [5.91, 10.02].

R2 = 0.7366. 73.66% of the differences between task completion times are due to the change in word color congruency.

I reject the null hypothesis; changing words from congruent to incongruent colors significantly increases the amount of time it takes participants to name the color the words are displayed in. This result was as expected.
